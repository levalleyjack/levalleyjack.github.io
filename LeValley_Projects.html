P2.3.2 Westpoint Bridge Designer Project

The goal of my teammate and I’s project, finished 3/19/19 for our Principles Of Engineering class, was to design and test a stable and low cost 2-lane bridge using the West Point Bridge Designer Software. My responsibility was to draw up two different designs considering the stability of the bridge, the cost, and the material that the bridge would be made of. I would have to decide the effectiveness of a pier and its cost, along with which material and which cross-section to use to fit each problem and it’s situation. My teammate and I built a decision matrix using average values and details from the feedback of the software which lead us to choosing my first design. I worked lastly on our documentation, explaining why we chose our bridge design and why it worked the best out of all of the others considering composition and the shape of it. All in all, I learned a lot what goes into designing a bridge and what to consider when creating future ones.

"Yodapie" Chatbot Project

The objective of our project was to create a chatbot based off of the character from the Star Wars films, “Yoda”, many of whom know to speak in an unusual pattern. He has been programmed to respond in his unique speech pattern to inputs related to topics about Star Wars and it’s universe. For many of the messages the user sends, they did not exactly fit the input responses our chatbot was looking for in its series of if statements. When this happened, we requested for the chatbot to ask another artificial intelligence to see what the intent of the user’s message is, using an API known as Wit.ai. Using Wit.ai, we were able to train an artificial intelligence to recognize any questions related to the Star Wars universe and then communicate back to our bot that these questions are indeed Star Wars related and that our bot should give the user a response addressing something in the realm of Star Wars. In this project, I learned many skills including how to use an API, make requests with JSON, and further progressed my knowledge of Java. For the project, I was able to write the statements that allowed our bot to give back feedback from the user’s input and I took part in training the AI aspect of the bot. My team overcame the challenge of getting our Java code to communicate to Wit.ai over HTTP.
